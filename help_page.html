Fitness tips and tricks

Sets & Reps

  • '3x(8-12)' means 3 sets of 8-12 reps
  • A rep is one complete motion of an exercise
  • A set is a group of completed reps
  • You should be resting a minimum of 2-3 minutes between each set

    • Diet

  • Food
    - It is vital that you get enough protein in order to repair those muscle tears and ultimately gain muscle!
    - Roughly 2g per kg of bodyweight is ideal.
    - Some protein-rich foods include beef, chicken, tuna, eggs and low-fat yoghurt.
    - It may be worth investing in some protein powder to help you accomplish your daily protein goal!
  • Water
    -It is important that you consume a minimum of 2-3 litres of water a day

    • Cardio

  • Whilst the focus of the fitness programme is muscle building, it is very important that you do aerobic exercise every week.
  • Building muscle is great, but so is your cardiovascular health!
  • Cardiovascular exercises include running, hiking, rowing, skipping and boxing.

    • Exercises explained

    Compound

    • Deadlift
      - Keep feet slightly closer than shoulder-width apart
      - Keep a neutral spine, hip hinge & push bum backwards
      - Grip the bar just outside of your knees and lower the bar
      - Then push up through your feet
    • Squats
      - Create a ridge with the shoulder blades for the bar to rest on
      - Keep feet rougly shoulder-width apart
      - Squat down to at least 90°
      - Then push up through your feet
    • Bench press
      - Keep head on the bench and a slight arch in the back
      - Ensure elbows are not too flared out
      - Control the bar to just below the nipple line
      - Then explosively push up

    Chest

    • Chest flies
      - Bring arms together, squeezing the pecs
      - Control the rep down, allowing the pecs to stretch
      - Restrict use of shoulders or biceps
      - Can use peck deck machine, cables or dumbbells
    • Incline DB press
      - Set the bench to an incline of about 30°
      - Ensure elbows are not too flared out
      - Control the dumbbells down towards the chest
      - Then explosively push up
    • Bench press
      - Keep head on the bench and a slight arch in the back
      - Ensure elbows are not too flared out
      - Control the bar to just below the nipple line
      - Then explosively push up

    Triceps

    • Skull crushers
      - Bring weight to just behind the head
      - Drive weight up throught the triceps
      - Can use a flat or inclined bench
      - Can use an easy bar, dumbbells, smith machine or cables
    • Tricep Pushdowns
      - Keep elbows tucked by your side
      - Push down and squeeze the triceps at the bottom of the rep
      - Can use rope or straight bar cable attachment
    • Tricep dips
      - Lower to a 90° bend at the elbow
      - Push up and straighten the arms
      - Can use resistance bands if it is too dificult
      - Can use added weight if it is too easy

    Shoulders

    • Shoulder press
      - Set the bench to an incline of just less than 90°
      - Ensure elbows are not too flared out
      - Control the dumbbells down to top of shoulder
      - Then explosively push up
    • Lateral raises
      - Start dumbbells by your side
      - Bends arms slightly inward & drive dumbbells up
      - Can use dumbbells, cables or side delt machine
      - Can use a bench for extra stability
    • Rear delt flies
      - Set the bench to an incline of about 45°
      - Keep a slight bend in the elbow as you do the flies
      - Can use dumbbells or rear-delt machine
      - Or can do rear-delt cable flies
    • Barbell shrugs
      - Hold barbell in front of you, keeping arms straight
      - Squeeze the traps as you bring the bar
      - Try to limit any momentum
      - Can use dumbbells too

    Back

    • Pull ups
      - Hang on to bar with knuckles facing outward
      - Pull yourself up until chin is above the bar
      - If it is too difficult, can use resistance bands
      - If it is too easy, can use added weight
    • Lat pulldown
      - Grip the attachment fairly wide apart
      - Lean slightly back as you pull down towards the top of the chest
      - Drive your elbows down towards the floor
      - Allow shoulders to round slightly at the top of the rep
    • Seated rows
      - Lean forward to allow a stretch in the lats
      - Drive elbows back whilst leaning back slightly
      - Try to limit any momentum
      - Choose the most comfortable attachment for you
    • Bent over rows
      - Lean forward and maintain a neutral spine
      - Drive elbows back and control weight down
      - Try to limit any momentum
      - Can use a barbell, smith machine or dumbbells

    Biceps

    • Hammer curls
      - Grip dumbbells with knuckles facing outwards
      - Squeeze at the top of rep & control down
      - Keep elbows still throughout movement
      - Can do these seated for extra stability
    • Seated bicep curls
      - Set the bench to an incline of about 70-80°
      - Squeeze at the top of rep & control down
      - Keep elbows and shoulders still
      - Flex tricep at the bottom of rep for a full bicep stretch
    • Preacher curls
      - Set the bench to an incline of about 70-80°
      - Lay one elbow on the bench
      - Squeeze at the top of rep & control down
      - Can use a preacher curl machine

    Quads

    • Squats
      - Create a ridge with the shoulder blades for the bar to rest on
      - Keep feet rougly shoulder-width apart
      - Squat down to at least 90°
      - Then push up through your feet
      - Can do smith machine or pendulum squats
    • Leg extensions
      - Adjust the seat to avoid a gap between the bum and seat
      - Extend legs explosively
      - Control down until legs are at 90°
      - Restrict momentum by keeping upper legs still
    • Leg press
      - Start with quite low on the pad
      - Control weight down and push through the quads
      - Keep tension by not locking legs out
      - Go as deep as possible without rounding the back
    • Bulgarian split squats
      - Rest one foot on the bench and put the other in front
      - Ensure knee is at 90° at bottom of movement
      - Control the weight down and then drive up
      - Can use dumbbells or a smith machine

    Hamstrings

    • Hamstring curls
      - Adjust the seat to avoid a gap between the bum and seat
      - Bring heels towards to bum (at least 90°)
      - Control weight until legs are straight
      - Can do them lying or on the seated machine
    • Romanian Deadlifts (RDLs)
      - Hinge at the hips and keep a small bend in the knees
      - Bring weight down until a stretch in the hamstrings
      - Keep a neutral spine and drive up
      - Can use a barbell, smith machine or dumbbells

    Thighs and calves

    • Adductor machine
      - Bring pad as wide as it is comfortable
      - Keep feet in neutral position
      - Bring weight in and squeeze
      - Control weight back out
    • Calf raises
      - Stand on some kind of elevated platform
      - Control weight down
      - Push weight up through the calves
      - Can use smith machine or calf raise machine
